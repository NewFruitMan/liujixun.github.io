RC Relaxation Oscillator


A 2M-Hz RC Relaxation Oscillator Based on Self-Regulated Voltage Reference

 

As the Crystal Oscillator is not suitable for VLSI circuits nowadays due to its size, a cheaper opportunity is to design a RC oscillator for intermediate frequency application. However, the precision is poor for conventional RC Relaxation Oscillator.
In this research, a self-regulated voltage supply is ultilized in order to adapt to wide range of input voltage(2.5V-5.5V). The temperature and fabrication variations are also compensated using specific logic of control. The final version could achieve a frequency variation less than 0.25% and a duty cycle variation less than 0.5%.

Voltage References


A Reference System Used For IoT Applications

 

The innovation to design such a voltage reference system is to combine the merits of different types of voltage references and decrease the required power as well as the area, which is benefitial to the IoT applications.
In this research, current controlled voltage reference and bandgap voltage reference are combined as a system to provide several low voltage power supplies(Vref < 2V). The system of voltage references could be trimmed with digital inputs. In this research, I design the layout of the circuit and another PhD schoolmate is mainly responsible for the schematic idea and simulations. The circuit has been taken for a tape-out.
